## Culture 
This is a big one for me. Programing is fun, it can be like art. Developing with a group of people for a shared goal can be even nicer. But, for some reason, mostly it's not. Something gets lost when the pressure to perform as an indevidual and as a group becomes the main focus. This isn't new, but still it happens all over.

So, how can the experience of writing software together can enhance and make us happier?

## CI/CD 
The CI/CD process in the company says a lot about the engineering culture in the
company, and how fun will it be to develop code there. This sounds like a
small detail but, from my experience, it's a huge deal:
* First and foremost: it's fun to deploy the code after you finish writing it.
    Waiting for a release is ok, but not as fun.
* It says a lot about the engineering culture and quality in the company.
    Developing and maintaining a good CI/CD flow requires a quite a lot of
    skills and effort, that eventually should pay off, but a some companies
    (most actually) have a hard time allocating resources to it. It's not a
    feature, and it requires quite a lot of effort. To me, it feels like failing
    to make the "right" technical descision in this field will manifest itself
    in other areas of the code base.

Questions:
* What is your deploy cycle? Are you deploying multiple times a day or once every 4 weeks?
